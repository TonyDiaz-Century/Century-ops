— Commercial membrane roofing

Why we install PVC,
not TPO.

Most Bay Area commercial roofers install TPO by default because it's cheaper. We don't. After 30 years on Bay Area roofs, we install PVC — the membrane that actually holds up to California sun, chemical exposure, and ponding water. Here's the side-by-side.

PVC — what we install
TPO — what we don't
UV & heat resistance
Chemically stable under California sun for 25–30+ years.
Field studies show accelerated degradation in high-UV climates.
Chemical exposure
Resists kitchen grease, HVAC oils, animal fats — critical for restaurants & commercial.
Susceptible to chemical damage; voids many warranties.
Seam strength
Hot-air-welded seams chemically fuse; weld is stronger than the membrane.
Welds work, but narrower welding window and more field-seam failures over time.
Ponding water
Designed for ponding conditions — common on Bay Area flat roofs.
Most TPO warranties exclude or limit ponding water claims.
Real-world lifespan
25–30 years documented on installed roofs.
Industry-quoted 15–20 years; field failures often earlier.
Up-front cost
Roughly 15–25% more per square.
Cheaper today. More expensive over the building's lifespan.

Came to us looking for a TPO bid? Great — let's walk your roof and talk through whether PVC, modified bitumen, or a hybrid approach is right for your building. No pressure, just a straight answer.
